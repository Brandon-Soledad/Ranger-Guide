Introduction

This guide isn't a wiki. I won't be explaining each individual Ranger ability since individually each ability is rather simple. This will be more of a focus on the possible ability combinations and an explanation of the more vague abilities like weapon combo's and finishers that are beneficial to a PVE scenario.

High Level Overview (PVE)

Weapon Choices

You have two options as a Ranger Longbow or Shortbow.

The weapon combo finishers are applied by a sequence of auto attacks and these finisher abilities vary based on the weapon you are using.

Longbow
Shortbow

These are the unique finishers that each bow provides.

  • Longbow Finishers:
    • Wound: Applies a debuff to decrease healing by 5%. This stacks 10 times for a maximum of 50% healing reduction.
    • Stagger: Applies staggered to your target, which reduces their evasion by 25% for 6 seconds. Subsequent applications extend the duration up to 15 seconds.
  • Shortbow Finishers:
    • Bleed: Applies a bleed that deals 15% of your physical power every second for 6 seconds.
    • Snare: Applies a movement speed debuff that reduces movement speed by 25% per stack (Can trigger diminishing returns which makes the target immune for 10 seconds to CC).

Both weapon types share some of the same finishers that provide extra support/utility:

Shared Finishers
  • Shared Finishers:
    • Keen Edge: Finishers and Extended Finishers have 25% increased critical strike chance. This bonus increaes to 50% for 10 seconds after casting an ability. Stacks 2 times.
    • Refreshing Flowthrough: All of your Finishers and Extended Finishers restore a % of your maximum mana for 10 seconds after casting an ability. Stacks 2 times.
    • Ward: Grants 5% Block Chance. Finishers and Extended Finishers grant temporary Health equal to 1% of your Maximum Health. Temporary Health granted increases to 2% of your Maximum Health after casting an Ability. Stacks up to two times.
    • Rythmic Swings Every Finisher and Extended Finisher increases your chance of a Deadly Finisher or Deadly Extended Finisher by 25%. Stacks 2 times.
    • Crushing Blows Finishers, Extended Finishers, Deadly Finishers, and Deadly Extended Finishers have 10% increased base damage and 10% increased critical damage.
    • Inner Fire Finishers and Extended Finishers increase Health Regeneration, Mana Regeneration, and Stamina Regeneration by (0.02% of max HP/1/1) per tick. Stacks up to two times. Deadly Finishers and Deadly Extended Finishers grant 2 stacks and restore an additional 1% Maximum HP, Mana, and Stamina.
Which weapon and finisher combo's do you pick?
Shortbow

You really can't go wrong with either. If you're in a single target scenario where the TTK is high, the Shortbow will usually be better as your Barrage will be up more often since its CD is reduced by the amount of autos on a target that's Bleeding. The shortbow also makes a better use of our bow mastery:

This makes it so that every time you proc a finisher your Barrage and Scattershot CD are reduced by 3 seconds, with the high attack speed of Shortbows you can proc finishers quick and you can weave in more autos during the duration that a target is bleeding.

Longbow

Longbows provide much higher Physical Power than a Shortbow, so auto for auto the Longbow results in more damage. However the Longbow attack speed is much lower than a shortbow. In an AoE scenario and in a group I'd say the Longbow is better, each individual AoE ability will deal more raw damage so you reduce the TTK on AoE grinds. Keep in mind this is based on feeling, there is no reliable way to measure DPS.

Finishers and your Primary Weapon

For your secondary weapon you ALWAYS go Greatsword. There is really no benefit to going anything else at the moment. If you're using a Shortbow you want to use Bleed and Wound on your Greatsword. This will allow you to use Enhance Armament: Weight to apply snares.

If you're using a Longbow you have a bit more flexibility you can use Wound and Bleed on your Greatsword. This will allow you to use Enhance Armament: Weight to apply snares. Alternatively you can use Snared on your Greatsword and use Enhance Armament: Barbs to apply bleeds.

For both weapon types you want to use Refreshing Followthrough this will help you a ton if you are having mana issues and your Bard is not providing mana. If your Bard is an absolute machine and keeping your mana up at all times then you can swap Refreshing Followthrough with Keen Edge to increase your finisher and deadly finisher critical chance by up to 50%. Ranger Skill Tree

This is overall a balanced skill trees for all scenarios, If you want you can remove a point off Mark of the Raven and into Hasting Snipe if you're doing single target for increased attack speed. But I'd recommended keeping Mark of the Raven as it's a good way to provide quick single target burst which scales with the targets missing HP.

The big things here for AoE are Lightning Reload, Explosive Thundering Shot, Concentrated Scattershot, and Vinefield. These are your bread and butter abilities when it comes to AoE damage You will want to spec into Concentrated Scattershot to charge it which will increase the damage. and Multi-Scatter Shot which will give you an additional cast of Scattershot. Abilities like Raining Death and Air Strike are good as well but generally fillers when your main abilities are on CD. You will also want to spec into Mortal Headshot which deals 50% more damage to mobs bellow 50% HP.

AoE Rotation
Once you find an AoE grind and you have the mobs grouped up the following is a general rotation you can follow.

    1. Scattershot/Vinefield: If you are using Enhance Armament: Barbs You want to start with Concentrated Scattershot to apply a Bleed to as many targets as possible. If you have Enhance Armament: Weight you can open up with Vinefield from the start.
    2. Vinefield/Scattershot If you previously opened up with Scattershot to apply Bleed you want to use Vinefield to apply Hermorraging to as many targets as possible. If you started with Vinefield then you can use Concentrated Scattershot.
    3. Explosive Thundering shot/Lightning Reload Here you want to use Explosive Thundering Shot in combination with Lightning Reload. Lightning Reload will allow you to cast an ability shot for free. So cast Lightning Reload first then do a double Explosive Shot. This provides a lot of instant burst damage.
    4. Raining Death From here all that's left is your filler abilities you can cast Raining Death while waiting for Explosive Thundering Shot and Scattershot to finish recharging both shots.
    5. Air Strike If Explosive Thundering Shot and Scattershot are not up at this point or if you just want to. You can cast Air Strike.

Single Target Rotation
Once you find an AoE grind and you have the mobs grouped up the following is a general rotation you can follow.

    1. Vinefield: Open up with Vinefield to apply Bleed.
    2. Snipe Cast Snipe after Vinefield to apply Shaken this will reduce the targets physical mitigation by 15% and we will also proc Hermorraging from the previous Bleed applied by Vinefield.
    3. Explosive Thundering shot Here you want to use Explosive Thundering Shot to keep bursting down the targets HP.
    4. Barrage/Lightning Reload/Mark of the Raven The targets HP at this point should be lower which is good for Mark of the Raven as it will do more damage the more HP the target is missing. You want to apply Mark of the Raven then cast Lightning Reload into a double Barrage this will do a ton of damage.
    5. Headshot Here you want to cast Headshot this will apply Wound to the target ideally you should be specced into Mortal Headshot to deal additional damage to the target once it's lower than 50% HP.
